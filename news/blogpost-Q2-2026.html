Lost Post Games: Blog Post - Q2 2026 Quarterly Review

July 1, 2026

We Went To Origins!

Can you believe we’re already half way through 2026? I’ve been working hard on Tricks of War and even got to go to Origins Game Fair in Columbus, Ohio to playtest a couple weeks ago. It went pretty well and I got some great feedback from all the people who came out to play with the Unpub Network. Let’s see what Log Post Games is up to…

Origins Game Fair

I happened to go on vacation the week before Origins so my prep got a little chaotic, but getting to playtest at Origins was great and also the first public playtest for Tricks of War. Up until now it’s been friends that I’ve seen play, so this was a pretty big moment and I got some awesome feedback. We’re currently still seeing an issue with keeping track of all that is going on in the game since multiple tricks at once can get pretty complicated, but that’s some design that’s getting worked on. I don’t have much from Origins exactly since I forgot to take any pictures, but it was a great time and I’m hopeful for more of these chances.

Pittsburgh Playtest

If you happen to be in Pittsburgh, I’ll also be bringing Tricks of War to Pittsburgh Playtest July 19th and August 23rd! I haven’t been able to bring Tricks of War to this event yet, but this is a great way to grab some more public feedback especially after whatever changes come out of Origins.

The Plan

Alright, so that’s what I’ve been up to, but where is this going? I want to run the kickstarter for Tricks of War in February 2027 and we still have a ways to go before we’re ready for that. Here’s where I’m currently at with the game:

  • Artwork is progressing: I’ve got an artist starting the sketches for each of the 12 monarch cards in the game and I’ve been working on drawing the sketches for the non-monarch suit art. I’m also trying to finish up the art for the frame on the front of the card. Of course this all needs to be carved eventually, but I need to know what to carve first.
  • Playtesting is going great! People are intrigued by the idea of the game, and are having fun. Work needs to be done here to solve some problems and make improvements. Important Note: deck-building might be removed entirely from the game?
  • Kickstarter ideas? I haven’t put much thought into the actual Kickstarter, but I know I definitely want some cool rewards. The art is meant to be a really cool focal point of the game, and hand carving the art means we can end up with some really cool prints. I also have a good amount of focus on history and I find myself learning a lot just working with the theme of the game. I want to make some kind of zine that details all of the inspirations for the game. This all comes much much later though.

I think that’s all my thoughts for this quarter, so go check out Tricks of War playtesting!

- Jess Case, Founder of Log Post Games
