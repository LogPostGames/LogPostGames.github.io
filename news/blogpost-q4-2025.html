Log Post Games: A Look Ahead - Q4 2025 Quarterly Review

Date: October 7, 2025

Welcome to the first-ever Quarterly Review for Log Post Games!

This post officially marks the launch of our revamped studio website and the beginning of a new tradition of transparency and progress. If you don’t know me, I’m Jess Case, a solo developer here at Log Post Games. My goal is to pull back the curtain on Log Post Games every three months. You'll hear about what I’ve built, what I’ve broken (and fixed!), and what’s next for the projects.

Since we're just getting started with this new format, this review is less about looking back and more about setting the course for the next three months. Let's talk about what's coming up in Q4 2025!

The Log Post Games Mission: Q4 2025 Focus

This quarter is all about applying our core value to "Commit to the Bit" by diving deep into the core content for both a digital and a physical project.

Project 1: Captain’s Log (Digital Narrative Game) üßë‍üíª

Development on the unannounced narrative game, currently codenamed Captain’s Log, has been entirely focused on core systems—the nuts and bolts of the game that will power the story. Now, I shift our focus to the content that matters most: the story and the brain teasers.

My primary goals for this quarter are:

  • Draft the Narrative Core: Completing the first full draft of the Captain’s Log story—from beginning to end. This will give us the blueprint we need to create and put the jumbled pieces of the story together.
  • Puzzle Implementation: Getting the first two playable puzzles ready within the engine. In Captain’s Log, you’ll be tracking down the truth and you’ll have to solve puzzles to uncover that truth.

Project 2: Tricks of War (Physical Card Game) üÉè

A sort of side project to Captain’s Log is Tricks of War is a multiplayer trick-taking and deck-building card game inspired by the wars of England’s Renaissance period.

Our goal for this quarter is simple: Keep Playtesting. We'll continue gathering feedback from local players to refine the core rule set to ensure every card feels like a powerful, tactical choice.
