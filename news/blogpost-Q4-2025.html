Dialing In - Q4 2025 Quarterly Review

January 1, 2025

Log Post Games: Blog Post - Q4 2025 Quarterly Review

2025 closed out with a whirlwind of activity at Log Post Games. While much of the work this quarter happened "under the hood," it has set the stage for a massive 2026. Here is a look at what we accomplished in the final months of the year.

The New Digital Front Door

The biggest public milestone was the official launch of the Log Post Games website. As a solo studio, having a dedicated space to showcase our philosophy is a game-changer. It’s more than a portfolio, it’s the hub where our community and future partners can see exactly what we’re building and why.

Captain’s Log: The Narrative Takes Shape

While Captain's Log didn’t see much in-engine work this quarter, the story really started to take shape. I’ve been spending time working on the high-level narrative for the voyage to Teegarden b. This is helping map out the entire game, as in-engine work can’t continue without knowing the direction of the story. Since this is a narrative-based game, focusing on the writing is a priority.

I also upgraded the project to Unity 6000.0.60f1. Our previous version (6000.0.23f1) had a known security vulnerability in built games, making this a quick but necessary upgrade for future player safety.

Warp Step: New Project Reveal

I am thrilled to announce a new project currently codenamed Project: Warp Step.

  • The Pitch: A small-scale, high-intensity roguelite with a great focus on movement and positioning.
  • The Team: While this is still a solo venture, I’m collaborating with a talented artist to give this game a distinct visual identity.
  • The Goal: We are aiming for a Summer 2026 release.

This project offers a chance to exercise different design muscles while Captain's Log continues its long-term development.

Tricks of War: Project Status

Being transparent means sharing the stalls as well as the wins. There was no new playtesting for Tricks of War this quarter. With the website launch and the birth of Project: Warp Step, I had to prioritize other areas. We’ll be looking at how this fits into the 2026 roadmap in the coming months.

Looking Ahead to Q1 2026

2026 is the year Log Post Games goes from "concept" to "active developer". My goals for the next three months are:

  • First playable prototype for Project: Warp Step.
  • Finishing the outline for Captain's Log and getting some scripting started for the game’s opening.
  • Holding a playtest or two for Tricks of War.

Thank you for following along on this solo-dev journey. Let’s build something memorable.

- Jess Case, Founder of Log Post Games
